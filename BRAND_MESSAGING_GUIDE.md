## Voice & Tone Guidelines

### Do:
✅ Be direct and confident
✅ Lead with outcomes, not features
✅ Use concrete language ("capture," "convert," "retain")
✅ Emphasize what AI *does*, not what it *suggests*
✅ Talk about leakage, demand, and operational layers
✅ Use healthcare-specific context

### Don't:
❌ Say "CRM" unless comparing/contrasting
❌ Over-promise unfinished features
❌ Use generic SaaS language ("powerful," "seamless," "intuitive")
❌ Lead with technology stack
❌ Bury outcomes in feature lists
❌ Use "beta" or "work in progress"

## Common Objections & Responses

**"We already have [EHR/CRM]"**
→ "Those handle documentation and billing. VantageAI is the operational layer that captures demand *before* it reaches your EHR."

**"Our staff won't adopt another tool"**
→ "This isn't another tool—it's the system that makes their existing work automated. Less clicking, more patient time."

**"AI sounds risky with PHI"**
→ "PHI redaction is automatic. Role-based access. Full audit trails. Compliance is built in, not bolted on."

**"What if the AI makes mistakes?"**
→ "Agents take safe actions—drafts, not finals. Every execution is logged and auditable. Human-in-the-loop where it matters."
